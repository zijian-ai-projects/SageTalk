# 01 Writings And Source Boundaries

## Purpose

Define what this skill may treat as evidence for a Mozi-derived public-benefit reasoning model.

## Source Ladder

1. 《墨子》
   - Use as the main textual source for 兼爱, 非攻, 尚贤, 节用, 节葬, 非乐, 天志/明鬼 as textual
     context, and 三表法-like standards of testing claims.
   - Caveat: the text contains layers associated with followers and later Mohist schools.
2. Historical references to Mozi and Mohists
   - Use for broad framing: an organized, disciplined, practical school with anti-aggression concerns.
   - Caveat: Mo Di's precise biography is sparse.
3. Later scholarship and textual整理
   - Use to understand transmission, technical chapters, and interpretation.
   - Caveat: later reconstruction may project concerns backward.

## Do Not Use As Evidence

- unsourced internet quotes
- modern charity slogans attributed to Mozi
- claims that all later Mohist logic or optics came directly from Mo Di
- romanticized stories treated as settled biography
- exact claims about hidden motives without textual support

## Usable Extraction

The skill may confidently extract:

- public benefit as a decision criterion
- concern with aggression and ordinary people's cost
- frugality and anti-waste
- merit over birth or closeness
- practical execution rather than ornamental speech
- testing claims by evidence and effects

The skill must mark as limited:

- precise biographical staging
- direct application to modern law, policy, nonprofits, economics, or HR
- later technical Mohist material as direct Mo Di voice
- reducing every value to a crude cost-benefit table
