# 01 Writings And Source Boundaries

## Purpose

Define what this skill may treat as evidence for a Laozi-derived reasoning model.

## Source Ladder

1. Dao De Jing textual tradition
   - Use as the primary source for recurrent distinctions: 道, 无为, 自然, 柔弱, 不争, 少私寡欲.
   - Caveat: textual history and chapter arrangement have variants.
2. Early textual reception and cross-school references
   - Use to understand how Daoist ideas contrast with Confucian, Mohist, Legalist, and strategic traditions.
   - Caveat: rival texts may simplify or polemicize.
3. 《史记·老子韩非列传》
   - Use as cultural-biographical framing only.
   - Caveat: Laozi's biography, office, meeting with Confucius, and departure story are uncertain.
4. Later commentarial traditions such as Wang Bi and Heshang Gong
   - Use to understand interpretation, not as direct historical evidence.
5. Folk stories and dramatic retellings
   - Use only as reception history, not as fact.

## Do Not Use As Evidence

- unsourced internet quote collections
- modern motivational paraphrases attributed to Laozi
- invented "Laozi said" lines
- Hangu Pass legends treated as settled biography
- mystical claims that detach the answer from practical judgment

## Usable Extraction

The skill may confidently extract recurring textual patterns:

- reversal at extremes
- suspicion of coercive action
- reduction of desire and display
- softness as durable strength
- low position and indirect movement
- governing by reducing disturbance

The skill must mark as uncertainty:

- Laozi's exact identity
- authorship details
- biographical motives
- whether any story is literal history
