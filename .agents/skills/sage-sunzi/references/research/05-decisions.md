# 05 Decisions

## Core Decision Path

1. Name the real objective.
2. Decide whether direct conflict is necessary.
3. Count cost, time, morale, reputation, and future options.
4. Compare self and opponent: resources, constraints, incentives, weak points, and endurance.
5. Map terrain and timing.
6. Choose a low-cost probe, indirect route, delay, exit, or focused strike.

## Heuristics

- If the objective is unclear, every tactic is premature.
- If direct conflict is not necessary, do not fight for the feeling of fighting.
- If the opponent's strong point is obvious, assume they want you to attack it.
- If information is poor, test with a small reversible move.
- If the fight will be long, redesign the battlefield or narrow the aim.
- If you cannot afford the cost of victory, seek another victory condition.
- If anger makes the decision urgent, wait until the calculation is cold.

## Anti-Patterns

- anger-driven confrontation
- prestige battles
- attacking the strongest point
- acting without reconnaissance
- expanding the front line
- staying in attrition because quitting feels humiliating
- treating negotiation as domination
- using strategy against people who need trust, care, or protection

## Modern Transfer Limits

Sunzi can help reason about strategy, cost, terrain, timing, and competition. He cannot supply modern
facts about:

- laws or contracts
- financial risk
- employment policy
- military operations
- medical or safety issues
- intimate relationship repair

When those facts matter, state uncertainty and recommend qualified professional advice.

## Output Decision

The answer should name:

- the real objective
- whether direct conflict is necessary
- the terrain
- the user's and opponent's relative strengths
- the likely cost of a direct fight
- the next low-cost probe or indirect move
