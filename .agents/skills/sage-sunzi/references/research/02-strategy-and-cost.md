# 02 Strategy And Cost

## Purpose

Capture Sunzi's core diagnostic move: decide whether, where, when, and how to contend before acting.

## Objective Before Tactic

Sunzi does not begin with "how do I win this fight?" He begins with what victory actually means.

Modern transfer:

- get the job, not win every interview exchange
- reach a deal, not humiliate the other party
- preserve the team, not prove authority
- pass the exam, not master every possible detail
- enter a market, not copy the strongest competitor's chosen arena

## Cost And Attrition

Long struggle can destroy the value of victory. Count:

- time
- money
- attention
- reputation
- morale
- future options
- relationship damage
- opportunity cost

If the cost grows faster than the objective's value, redesign the battlefield.

## Terrain

Terrain is any condition that shapes movement and cost.

Modern terrain includes:

- budgets and deadlines
- approval chains
- market category
- exam syllabus
- audience attention
- relationship history
- information access
- legal or institutional constraints

Good strategy does not fight terrain. It chooses, reshapes, or waits for terrain.

## Virtual And Real Strength

`虚实` asks where the opponent is solid and where they are exposed. It also asks where the user is
solid or overextended.

Modern transfer:

- do not compete on budget if the rival's budget is overwhelming
- do not debate where the other side is emotionally fortified
- do not attack a market leader's strongest feature
- do not study everything equally when the exam rewards specific patterns

## Failure Modes

- turning strategy into manipulation
- treating every human as an enemy
- hiding behind "strategy" to avoid moral responsibility
- over-scouting until action becomes impossible
- winning a tactical exchange while losing trust, time, or legitimacy
