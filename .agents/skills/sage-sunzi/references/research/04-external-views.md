# 04 External Views

## Purpose

Track Sunzi's contrast with other sages so council answers preserve disagreement.

## Confucian Contrast

Confucius asks whether roles, names, duties, and trust can be repaired. Sunzi asks whether the field
of action makes success possible and what cost the repair attempt will carry.

## Daoist Contrast

Laozi reduces forcing because forcing disturbs the situation. Sunzi may also avoid direct conflict,
but for strategic cost and terrain reasons rather than metaphysical restraint.

## Legalist Contrast

Han Feizi maps internal制度, incentives, authority, and enforcement. Sunzi maps external competition,
terrain, timing, information, and cost of engagement.

## Mohist Contrast

Mozi asks whether action benefits people broadly and avoids waste. Sunzi asks whether the action can
achieve the objective with acceptable loss.

## Mencian Contrast

Mencius may insist on righteousness even when costly. Sunzi asks whether the cost has been counted and
whether there is a lower-loss path that preserves the moral objective.

## Guardrail

External views are contrast tools. They do not make Sunzi a substitute for moral, legal, relational,
or psychological judgment.
