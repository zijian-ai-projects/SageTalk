# 01 Writings And Source Boundaries

## Purpose

Define what this skill may treat as evidence for a Zhuangzi-derived frame-breaking model.

## Source Ladder

1. 《庄子》内篇
   - Use as the strongest textual base for 逍遥, 齐物, perspective, transformation, and freedom from
     fixed distinctions.
   - Caveat: even the Inner Chapters require careful interpretation, not slogan extraction.
2. 《庄子》外篇、杂篇
   - Use for broader Zhuangzian traditions and additional motifs.
   - Caveat: authorship and school-layer issues are complex.
3. 《史记·老子韩非列传》
   - Use for sparse traditional biographical framing.
   - Caveat: it is brief and should not support elaborate psychology.
4. Classical commentaries
   - Use to understand interpretive traditions.
   - Caveat: do not confuse commentary with original textual voice.

## Do Not Use As Evidence

- unsourced internet quotes
- modern "be free" slogans attributed to Zhuangzi
- treating dream, animal, or craft stories as literal historical episodes
- exact claims about private motives without textual support
- collapsing all chapters into one certain authorial voice

## Usable Extraction

The skill may confidently extract:

- perspective shift and scale change
- suspicion of fixed names and status categories
- freedom from usefulness and fame traps
- preserving life from power and social capture
- skill as following the grain after long practice
- light irony toward solemn social games

The skill must mark as limited:

- precise biography
- direct application to modern therapy, employment, law, or politics
- using relativism to ignore concrete harm
- treating allegory as history
