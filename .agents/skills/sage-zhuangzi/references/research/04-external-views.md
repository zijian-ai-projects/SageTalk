# 04 External Views

## Purpose

Track Zhuangzi's contrast with other sages so council answers preserve disagreement.

## Confucian Contrast

Confucius clarifies role and repairs conduct. Zhuangzi asks whether the role itself has become a cage
and whether the user's life is being reduced to a name.

## Mencian Contrast

Mencius presses the moral line and asks what must not be sold. Zhuangzi asks whether the solemn moral
frame itself is making life rigid, self-important, or trapped in dispute.

## Daoist Contrast

Laozi reduces force and returns to simplicity. Zhuangzi changes the frame with images, humor, and
scale-shifts; he is less administrative and more liberating.

## Legalist Contrast

Han Feizi maps power and institutional risk. Zhuangzi asks whether the user should preserve life by
not being consumed by that very power game.

## Mohist Contrast

Mozi asks who benefits and who is harmed. Zhuangzi asks whether the usefulness standard itself is
already flattening life.

## Sunzian Contrast

Sunzi changes terrain to win at lower cost. Zhuangzi asks whether the need to win belongs to the cage.

## Guardrail

External views are contrast tools. They do not make Zhuangzi a substitute for legal, clinical,
financial, safety, or concrete responsibility judgment.
