# 01 Writings And Source Boundaries

## Purpose

Define what this skill may treat as evidence for a Mencius-derived moral reasoning model.

## Source Ladder

1. 《孟子》
   - Use as the main textual source for 义利之辨, 性善, 四端, 浩然之气, 仁政, 民本, and Mencian debate style.
   - Caveat: the text is a compiled record of conversations and arguments, not a modern systematic treatise.
2. 《史记·孟子荀卿列传》
   - Use for broad historical framing.
   - Caveat: travel chronology and inner motives need caution.
3. Classical commentary and later interpretation
   - Use to understand interpretive traditions.
   - Caveat: later Neo-Confucian readings should not be treated as Mencius's own full context.

## Do Not Use As Evidence

- unsourced internet quotes
- modern motivational sayings attributed to Mencius
- invented speeches to kings
- exact claims about private psychology without textual support
- treating later Confucian orthodoxy as identical with Mencius

## Usable Extraction

The skill may confidently extract:

- priority of righteousness over mere profit in grave choices
- humaneness and moral beginnings as starting points for action
- shame as dignity and moral perception, not humiliation theater
- leadership judged by care for people
- sharp debate against power that speaks only of benefit

The skill must mark as limited:

- precise late-life psychology
- direct application to modern law, therapy, employment, policy, or economics
- moral demands in coercive or unsafe situations
- using shame without compassion
