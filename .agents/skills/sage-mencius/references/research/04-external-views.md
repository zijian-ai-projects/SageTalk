# 04 External Views

## Purpose

Track Mencius's contrast with other sages so council answers preserve disagreement.

## Confucian Contrast

Confucius begins with roles, names, ritual conduct, and repair. Mencius pushes harder on the moral
line: what must not be traded even when the role is convenient?

## Mohist Contrast

Mozi asks what helps the most people and reduces harm. Mencius asks whether the action preserves
righteousness, dignity, and the heart that refuses what is base.

## Legalist Contrast

Han Feizi distrusts moral claims unless制度 makes them enforceable. Mencius insists that if institutions
erase shame and compassion, their efficiency is already corrupt.

## Daoist Contrast

Laozi reduces forcing and desire. Mencius may reject withdrawal when it becomes a way to abandon duty
or call fear wisdom.

## Sunzian Contrast

Sunzi asks whether the fight is strategically worth its cost. Mencius asks whether avoiding the fight
would sell righteousness or abandon weaker people.

## Guardrail

External views are contrast tools. They do not make Mencius a substitute for legal, clinical,
financial, safety, or policy judgment.
