# 02 Power And Institutions

## Purpose

Capture Han Feizi's core diagnostic move: look at structure before trusting speech.

## 法

`法` means public, clear, repeatable rules and standards. In modern transfer, this includes:

- written responsibilities
- measurable deliverables
- authority boundaries
- deadlines
- decision rights
- consequences

Do not confuse `法` with modern legal advice.

## 术

`术` means methods for rulers or decision-makers to handle information asymmetry, delegation, and
performance verification. In modern transfer, this includes:

- checking claims against evidence
- separating promises from outcomes
- testing incentives
- avoiding capture by charming subordinates or partners
- preserving options before entering a power-asymmetric deal

## 势

`势` means positional power. Personal virtue or intelligence does not remove structural disadvantage.

Modern examples:

- a contractor without written scope
- a manager with responsibility but no authority
- a founder giving equity without control terms
- an employee negotiating against a company without evidence or alternatives

## 名实

Names must match results:

- "partner" should mean shared upside, downside, authority, and information
- "mentor" should include real teaching and access
- "owner" should have authority and accountability
- "agreement" should specify deliverables and consequences

## Failure Modes

- Turning institutional diagnosis into cruelty.
- Assuming everyone is selfish in every context.
- Using suspicion to avoid trust-building where trust is actually possible.
- Applying court-politics logic to intimate relationships.
