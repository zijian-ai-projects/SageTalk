# 04 External Views

## Purpose

Track Han Feizi's contrast with other sages so council answers preserve disagreement.

## Confucian Contrast

Confucius begins with role, trust, ritual, and self-cultivation. Han Feizi asks whether the system
lets good conduct survive. He distrusts arrangements that require virtue to patch incentives.

## Daoist Contrast

Laozi asks whether action is excessive and whether the user should reduce disturbance. Han Feizi asks
whether withdrawal simply leaves the user exposed to someone else's power.

## Mencian Contrast

Mencius protects moral courage and righteousness. Han Feizi asks whether righteousness without power,
evidence, and enforcement will be eaten by the structure.

## Mohist Contrast

Mozi evaluates public benefit and cost. Han Feizi evaluates enforceability: who can make the useful
thing actually happen and prevent defection?

## Sunzian Contrast

Sunzi maps terrain and timing for victory. Han Feizi maps internal制度, incentives, and authority that
decide whether a plan can be executed at all.

## Guardrail

External views are contrast tools. They do not make Han Feizi a full substitute for moral, relational,
legal, or psychological judgment.
