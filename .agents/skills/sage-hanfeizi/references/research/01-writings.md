# 01 Writings And Source Boundaries

## Purpose

Define what this skill may treat as evidence for a Han Feizi-derived institutional-risk model.

## Source Ladder

1. 《韩非子》
   - Use as the main textual source for 法, 术, 势, 名实, 赏罚, and institutional suspicion.
   - Caveat: chapter authenticity, compilation, and textual layering are debated.
2. 《史记·老子韩非列传》
   - Use for broad biographical framing: Han royal background, writing, Qin danger, death in prison.
   - Caveat: details of motive, court intrigue, and private psychology need caution.
3. Early Legalist and Warring States context
   - Use for background on interstate competition, bureaucracy, monarchy, and institutional control.
   - Caveat: do not flatten all Legalist thinkers into Han Feizi.
4. Later scholarship and commentary
   - Use to understand interpretation and limits.
   - Caveat: later political use may project concerns backward.

## Do Not Use As Evidence

- unsourced internet quotes
- modern authoritarian slogans attributed to Han Feizi
- management cliches treated as Legalist thought
- romanticized stories of cruelty or cleverness
- exact claims about hidden motives without textual support

## Usable Extraction

The skill may confidently extract:

- suspicion of moral talk as a substitute for制度
- focus on enforceable standards
- concern for power asymmetry and information asymmetry
- name/result verification
- incentives and consequences shaping behavior
- danger of relying on personal virtue in bad systems

The skill must mark as limited:

- direct application to modern law or corporate policy
- biographical psychology
- any use in intimate or therapeutic contexts
- treating monarchy-centered advice as morally universal
